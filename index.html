Always Available

  • Oyster Mushroom Log Kits $12

    I routinely offer an array of log kits that you can use to inoculate your own logs at home. You can easily inoculate 3-4 logs! These kits come with a pint of mushroom spawn, wax, a wax dauber and instructions. If you plan to inoculate more than a couple logs, let me know and we can expand the kit to match your needs!

  • Pasteurized Straw Bucket (uninoculated) $12

    This is perfect for a grower that has some mushroom spawn and just wants a bucket or two to experiment with. There may be a two day handling time for me to pasteurize straw in order to fulfill an order. These buckets are highly reusable!

  • Sterile Bird Seed Spawn Jars $4

    I offer quart jars currently. These are the exact jars I use. Each jar comes with a fresh air exchange lid and a ring. These jars are not inoculated and you will need a fully colonized jar or live culture to inoculate the jars. I will at times have fully colonized spawn jars for sale.

  • Food Grade Mushroom Cultivation Bucket $5

    These are what I use for my ready to grow buckets. They are repurposed frosting containers. The holes are drilled .75 inches in diameter, and are spaced to maintain moisture while providing ample space for cluster formation. You will receive an empty bucket with the lid and the holes drilled.

Remaining: Taragon 19
Blue Oyster 5

Updated 9/6/24

Thank you to those supporting the business with your purchases! I am a small start up and your purchases help me provide others the ability to grow their own food!

These buckets were put together 9/6 for a few events coming up including the Lilac Breeze Design Studio craft vendor fair and the West Bend Farmers Market. This cultivar is my personal favorite not only for its flavor but for how quickly it grows!

Tarragon oyster mushrooms ara a less common variety that isn't commercialy grown. They originate from the British Isles. The caps start as a dark tan and gradually turn to a cream color at they mature up to 8 inches across. The smell is where the tarragon oyster gets its name from. The tarragon plant and the oyster mushroom have an herbiness that could be compared to basil or marjoram.
